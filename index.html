Scheme Parameters

Complexity of Calcs — Guide

Very Simple A single calculation type with straightforward rules. Standard salary and service-based benefits only — no special factors, tranches, or scheme-specific complexity.
Simple One or two calculation types with minor special factors. Rules are well-documented with few exceptions or edge cases requiring interpretation.
Moderate Several calculation types with some scheme-specific complexity — for example multiple benefit tranches, revaluation bases, or benefit limits. Rules are clear but require careful implementation.
Complex Many calculation types with significant complexity — such as GMP equalisation, underpins, or hybrid benefit structures. Rules may contain ambiguities requiring trustee or legal clarification.
Very Complex Full suite of calculation types with multiple interacting complexity areas. Rules are likely to require significant clarification and may involve legislative uncertainty or bespoke benefit structures unique to the scheme.

Data Quality — Guide

Excellent Data is fully validated, complete, and consistent. No known issues. Can be used as-is without further investigation or cleansing.
Good Data is mostly clean with only minor gaps or anomalies. Minimal investigation needed before calculations can begin.
Average Some known gaps, inconsistencies, or missing fields. Data analysis and partial cleansing will be required as part of the analysis phase.
Poor Significant data issues including missing records, conflicting data, or format problems. Substantial investigation and remediation expected before calculations can begin.
Very Poor Major data problems requiring a significant remediation exercise. A dedicated data cleansing workstream will likely be needed before the calculation build can start in earnest.
